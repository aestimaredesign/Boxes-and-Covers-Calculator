How to Use This Calculator

1 Project Information: Enter project details like name, client, location, and date.

2 General Parameters: Set wall area, outlet density, wastage percentage, labor rates, and crew information.

3 Box Distribution: Adjust percentage distribution for different box types (must total 100%).

4 Unit Costs: Set individual costs for each box type and accessories.

5 Calculate: Click "Calculate" to generate the cost breakdown and labor requirements.

6 Export: Export results as PDF or Excel for professional use.

Calculation Formulas

Quantity Calculations:

Total Gangs = Wall Area × Outlets per m²

Box Quantities = Total Gangs × Distribution % × (1 + Wastage %)

Covers = Total Gangs × (1 + Wastage %)

Cost Calculations:

Material Cost = Quantity × Unit Cost

Labor Hours = Total Gangs ÷ (Productivity × Crew Size)

Labor Cost = Labor Hours × Labor Rate

Final Pricing:

Overhead Cost = Total Direct Cost × Overhead Rate

Profit = (Direct Cost + Overhead) × Profit Margin Rate

Final Price = Direct Cost + Overhead + Profit

Standard Box Types & Applications

Common Electrical Box Types:

  • Junction Box (4x4"): For wire connections and splices, typically surface-mounted
  • Utility Box: General purpose boxes for switches and outlets
  • Square Box: Deep boxes for multiple gang installations
  • Pull Box (8x8"): Large boxes for wire pulling and access points
  • Gutter: Wireways for running multiple circuits
  • Wireways (4x4", 6x6", 8x8"): Various sizes for different capacity needs

NEC 2023 Compliance Notes:

  • All boxes must be sized according to conductor fill requirements
  • Junction boxes must remain accessible after installation
  • Boxes in wet locations require weatherproof covers
  • Follow local amendments to NEC requirements
